# Echo

## Tagline

Before making a decision, hear from the people nobody thought to ask.

---

## Overview

Echo is an AI-powered Decision Blind Spot Detector.

Organizations frequently make decisions by consulting only the most visible or vocal stakeholders while overlooking groups that are indirectly affected.

Echo analyzes a proposed decision, identifies all affected stakeholders, simulates potential impacts across those groups, highlights conflicts of interest, and surfaces overlooked perspectives before the decision is implemented.

Echo does not determine whether a decision is right or wrong.

Instead, it reveals blind spots.

---

## Problem

Decision-makers often hear from:

* Executives
* Administrators
* Managers
* Vocal stakeholders

But they frequently miss:

* Disabled users
* Commuters
* Part-time workers
* Scholarship holders
* Parents
* Regional communities
* Other indirectly affected groups

These overlooked perspectives can create unintended consequences.

---

## Solution

A user uploads or enters a proposed decision.

Examples:

* Mandatory attendance policies
* Organizational changes
* Company policies
* Government proposals
* Institutional regulations

Echo then:

1. Discovers affected stakeholders.
2. Identifies hidden stakeholders.
3. Simulates potential impacts.
4. Highlights conflicting interests.
5. Generates stakeholder perspectives.
6. Reveals groups that may have been forgotten.

---

## Core Features

### Stakeholder Discovery

Automatically identifies direct and indirect stakeholders affected by a decision.

Example:

Decision:
Mandatory 85% attendance.

Stakeholders:

* Toppers
* Average students
* Hostel students
* Commuter students
* Disabled students
* Parents
* Professors
* Recruiters
* Scholarship holders

---

### Blind Spot Detection

Echo identifies stakeholders that decision-makers may not have considered.

Example:

Who Did We Forget?

* Disabled students
* Long-distance commuters
* Part-time workers
* Scholarship recipients

---

### Impact Analysis

Analyzes how each stakeholder group may be affected.

Outputs:

* Positive impact
* Negative impact
* Mixed impact

---

### Conflict Mapping

Highlights situations where one stakeholder benefits while another is negatively affected.

Example:

Higher attendance requirements

Benefits:

* Academic consistency

Harms:

* Internship participation
* Working students

---

### Stakeholder Voices

Users can click any stakeholder and hear a generated voice perspective.

Example:

Commuter Student

"I travel over two hours daily. This policy would make it difficult for me to participate in internships and maintain attendance requirements."

---

### Multilingual Perspectives

Echo generates stakeholder viewpoints from multiple Indian language communities.

Examples:

* Hindi-speaking student
* Tamil-speaking commuter
* Bengali parent
* Marathi working student

This ensures decisions are not evaluated solely through an English-speaking lens.
